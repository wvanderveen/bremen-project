Mistrust, racism and lack of privacy

The German authorities not only doubt that the women are telling the truth, but they also question the authenticity of the documents presented as proof from the pregnant women. They are digging into the women's past, interviewing their relatives and interrogating community members about the women's private lives, exposing them to the judgment of others.

Papers are everything, but not every paper counts

Papers upon papers are requested from the women: certificates of divorce, acknowledgement of paternity, school certificates, passports, etc. However, in the end, it is never enough and their papers are rejected. Instead, people from the embassies are employed to visit a person's home and interview their relatives. These are more trusted than the women's own words and certificates. As one person said: “You believe in paper and ink but you don't believe our papers!”

The system is racist!

Black mothers and their babies are treated differently from any white person. A lot of white people have children without being married — nobody cares about that. Then why does it become an issue for black women? Racism is the problem.

Everyone's Privacy Must Be Respected!

Privacy is important for everybody. Yet when it comes to black migrant women and their children, every stone is turned and every corner is searched to drag out their past. 

“The mother is supposed to know the father of her baby, not you choosing the father of the baby!”
Read full quote
“They did not put the father's name on the paper, they put the name of my ex-husband — that name they put on the paper as the father of the baby. After doing Vaterschaft — I do my Vaterschaft, I do everything — they did not put the name on the Vaterschaft, they went and put the name of my ex-husband. The two children I brought from Italy to Germany, they put their father's name on the new baby's name. I went there and said, ‘This man is not the father. The mother is supposed to know the father of her baby, not you choosing the father to the baby.’ Even if the woman is married before to the man, you are not supposed to put the man's name. At least you do Vaterschaft, I did everything, I said, ‘This is the father of the baby.’ Are you telling me that the woman don't know who impregnated her? You went ahead and put the others' father's name. They do not do it to only me, even many women who came with their children. That is how the Standesamt put their birth certificate, they put the father of their former babies' names. They add it to the new birth certificate. Then, they apply, they say, ‘Okay, we are going for verification to know if the name is submitted.’”

“She looked at the paper and said: ‘But you have three children. One, two, three — and you are not married?’”
Read full quote
“My man was there, that is the father for my child was there; we were there together. She was asking, ‘How...are you married? Were you married?’ I said, ‘No, I am not married.’ She look at the paper and said, ‘But you have these children, three children! One, two, three and you are not married?’ I said, ‘For your correction: my two children, my first two children, they are twins. I had a twin in one pregnancy. Then I have my daughter after the twin.’ ‘So, how can you say you are not married?’ I said, ‘I am not married. Look at the father of my child here, we have this child together, but we are not married. But we have a child together.’ She said, ‘Okay, and for the man — I don't doubt.’ The man was even asking him, ‘Okay, can you give me a proof that you are not married?’ So, she was: ‘Does it mean that this man you had the three children for doesn't have relationship with your family?’ The man I had these children for, that is in presence of this other man now, the man that you have this children for — does this mean that this man doesn't have relationship with your family? I said, ‘Of course. If I dated him for a while, before we had these children together and maybe we were together but we thought we will get married if it works but if it doesn't work you back out, you don't have to kill yourself there.’ I said, ‘So we are together.’ So, you have dated the man one year, two years, three years, your parent does not know him, it is not correct. You are wrong. My parents know him, they knew themselves, they actually — normally what we do at our place, if you are dating with somebody you could introduce the person to your parents. This is the man I want to be with. I am with this man. You know, maybe later, we will come and get married, then you go. And in some cultures, they don't even believe in marriage before children, before having children. You can have as many children as you want to have and then you can later come and officially do the marriage — that is pay the bride price of this person...The woman was putting it in my mouth — ‘I put it on you that you have must been married to this man.’ I said, ‘Well, the only thing I did was that I introduced this man to my family. We met, we sat down together. I introduce to my family, so my family know him.’ For me to say I dated somebody, we were friends and I have a child or children for him and my family does not know him, I cannot be saying the truth. I said my family know him. He knows my family members. But along the line, it didn't work so everybody parts ways here, but we have these children. These children have their life.”

Imprint

This website is the outcome of a collaboration between four activists of Together We Are Bremen and two researchers of the University of Bayreuth. The project was funded by the German Research Foundation (Deutsche Forschungsgemeinschaft, Grant #SCHR 981/10-2). The website development and design was carried out by William VanderVeen with support from Unnati Shukla. Felix Schramm from ag-prop supported and advised us throughout the website development process. Our workshops took place at LidiceHaus in Bremen.

Contact

You can contact Together We Are Bremen at shut-down-gottlieb-daimler@free-migration.org or you can write a message on Facebook, Instagram, or Twitter. For more information about the research project, you can reach us via email at born-twice@uni-bayreuth.de.

Donate

Together We Are Bremen needs regular donations, to help them to continue self-organization and the struggles for the right to stay and against racism in Bremen. You can donate to a private account or to the account of the association BreSoC (BremenSolidarityCenter). You can donate to the private account in case you don't need a receipt for your taxes. For TWAB, the private account means less administrative requirements. Send an email to contact@togetherwearebremen.org or send them a message on Facebook, Instagram, or Twitter for the account information.
